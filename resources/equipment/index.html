Caulking

After a couple years of testing, I’ve decided that Big Stretch is the only caulk worth using. Although it is expensive (~$6.00 a tube), it works where the others failed. This is especially true on the Bay, where buildings are normally old, wooden, and very exposed.

Ladders

  • 24-ft Werner Aluminum Extension Ladder
  • 8-ft Werner Fiberglass Stepladder.

Masking

I use a combination of tape, paper, and drop clothes.

For tape, I use 3M 2020 for general-use, and Green Frogtape for preventing paint-bleeds and getting ultra-sharp lines. For my general-purpose tape, I use 1’ and 1.5’ widths, for frogtape I only use 1'.

For paper, I use 9’ Masking Paper and a 3M Hand-Masker to apply it. Masking paper is used anywhere a painted surface and a non-painted surface intersect (like the body of the house with the deck).

Finally, drop clothes are used to cover large areas.

Spackling

I use 3M Spackle + Primer. It is the only spackle that I know of which doesn’t require priming after it’s dry.

Spraying

I use an airless sprayer, a Wagner GX06 2-Finger Gun, Titan hose, and variety of Titan tips.

I generally use a 515 Titan HEA tip to spray the body of the house. For the trim, windows, and doors I will generally use a 310 Titan Fine Finishing Tip. However I carry a wide range of tip sizes with me at all times to be ready for any situation.

Washing

For pressure washing I use a Simpson MegaShot 2700 PSI Pressure Washer, a Simpson Turbo Nozzle, 100-ft of Canpump Hose, and Powerfit Extension Wands.

For interior wall cleaning, I simply use a sponge, hot water, and Dawn Dishsoap.
